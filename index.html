About Me

I’m 38 years old and from Ukraine. I have lived in Denmark for the past six months since the beginning of the war in my home country. Before moving to Denmark I spent 14 years working for a state institution in the field of land/cadastral resources. First as Human Resources associate, however due to the circumstances in 2008 I became involved in the process of transferring information from physical sources to digital format (XML). This project took over two years and I enjoyed this type of work and I was offered the position of a Cadastral Registrar with the condition of studying at the university and obtaining a bachelor's degree in geodesy and land management.
I worked and studied at the same time. During the period of COVID-19 lockdown and the availability of free time, I became interested in programming and web development. The experience fascinated me so much that I realized I wanted to pursue this career for the rest of my life. I like the magic of creating something out of nothing....
My first learning platform was courses on Udemy, then a lot of video tutorials on YouTube. During my studies, I wanted to practice more, so my programmer friends gave me easy tasks to make websites, download pictures from Figma and sort these into folders, make small blocks layouts etc.

In autumn 2021 I entered The Rolling scope school from EPAM and successfully completed JS/FE PRE-SCHOOL 2022 (JAVASCRIPT). In February 2022 I received the certificate and I was meant to continue with my studies with a more complicated level and mentoring, however due to the war I had to leave this course but not my dream to become a web developer.

In Denmark, I applied and passed the selection at the coding school HackYourFuture and since October I have been studying there. Kolding is where I live so I go to Sprogskole. Despite Danish’s complexity of pronunciation and spelling, I enjoy the language and I want to learn it.

Currently, I am raising a 14-year-old daughter. I am very lucky because she is an incredibly talented, smart and creative child. With her, there is never a dull moment. In addition to drawing, playing computer games, reading books and playing computer games, she is also very fond of constructing Lego structures. At the time, I was convinced that people who work for Lego are the luckiest people in the world, and I still hold this belief today. Being able to do what you love every day is the best feeling in the world. There is nothing more fulfilling for me than to be part of a company that is changing the world for the better and I would love to be a part of it. Children are our future and through the whole Lego concept there is a great contribution to the development of the versatile values ​​of the growing generation. I’m super positive and motivated to touch this incredibly exciting process that requires constant creativity and studying. I am ready to work in a team or on my own initiative. I’m sociable, I love people, I have not lost faith in justice and I strongly believe that Russia will soon stop killing my nation, and I am ready to do my best to achieve success.

Navbar Effect | javaScript

              
                const fullImg = document.querySelector(".full-img");
                const smallImg = document.querySelectorAll(".gallery img");
                const modal = document.querySelector(".modal");
                
                // console.log(smallImg);
                smallImg.forEach(function (img) {
                  img.addEventListener("click", function () {
                    modal.classList.add("open");
                    fullImg.classList.add("open");
                
                    // Changin' the images dynamically
                    const originalQuality = img.getAttribute("alt");
                    fullImg.src = `img/full/${originalQuality}.jpg`;
                  });
                });
                
                modal.addEventListener("click", function (e) {
                  if (e.target.classList.contains("modal")) {
                    modal.classList.remove("open");
                    fullImg.classList.remove("open");
                  }
                });
